Our Partners

For this project, we have multiple partners and collaborators that will contribute their time and resources to ensure the success of The Expanded Experience. Since one of the main foci of this endeavor is to give museum-goers a more intimate interaction with the museum and a greater appreciation and understanding of the exhibits and artifacts on display, our first and biggest partner is the National Museum of African American Heritage and Culture. We plan to draw on their archives and store rooms for artifacts, primary source documents, and recordings that are not on display or normally accessible to the general public to give further weight to the exhibits on display in the main halls and galleries.

Our Partners

Another partner of this progress will be the United States Library of Congress. As the nation’s oldest federal institution, the Library of Congress is also the world’s largest library, housing collections and media from all around the world and is not bound by language or subject matter. Additional materials from this archive, not present at the NMAAHC, include primary source documents and audio recordings of conversations, interviews, news reports, and much more that will add to the corpus of supplemental material offered by the Expanded Experience. A added benefit to partnering with the Library of Congress is that most of the media housed there are open and available to the public, granting easy procurement for this project.
